The Underrated Films You Need to See

Every year, a handful of brilliant films slip quietly under the radar — and 2025 is no exception.

Hidden Gems ● 10 May 2025
“Sometimes the quietest films echo the loudest in your memory.” —Chloé Zhao on overlooked cinema

While big-budget blockbusters dominate headlines, some of the most powerful cinematic experiences this year haven’t come from box office giants — they’ve come from small, soulful stories that whisper instead of shout. These hidden gems don’t rely on spectacle to leave an impression; they rely on honesty, intimacy, and craft.

Take Glass Wings, a tender drama from debut director Elio Tran. Centered around a retired schoolteacher rediscovering herself through street photography in Hanoi, the film blends stillness with striking visual poetry. No explosions. No twists. Just the quiet miracle of seeing — and being seen.

Then there’s June In Reverse, a haunting, nonlinear tale about grief told entirely through voicemail messages and home videos. Shot on Super 8 and starring newcomer Amara Yusuf, it explores how time doesn’t heal — it distorts. Critics are calling it this year’s Aftersun, but with its own surreal voice.

Comedy gets its hidden gem too in *Left on Read*, a razor-sharp satire about influencer burnout, filmed entirely on phone screens. Hilarious and heartbreaking, it’s Eighth Grade for the TikTok generation, with a standout performance by Kayla Tran that’s already generating indie award buzz.

International cinema is also offering treasures. South Africa’s Dust Light, directed by Nomvula Seko, tells the story of a teenage girl navigating her township’s post-election tension. It’s tender, political, and utterly unforgettable. And from Argentina comes Panorama Azul, a sun-bleached coming-of-age road trip about queer friendship and final goodbyes.

These films might not trend or dominate streaming charts, but they stay with you — like a song you didn’t expect to love until it broke your heart a little.

By Priya Desai
